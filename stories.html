Case Study 01

Label Grabber

Unified shipping label management that turned a 4-hour daily task into 15 minutes.

The Problem

We sell on multiple platforms—StockX, our own Shopify, Veeqo for inventory management. Every day we ship hundreds of packages. The old process was brutal:

  1. Go to StockX, find the orders, download shipping labels one by one
  2. Go to Veeqo, do the same thing
  3. Try to match labels to the right products in the warehouse
  4. Print everything separately
  5. Hope nothing gets mixed up

This took hours. Every. Single. Day. And when you're processing 500+ orders daily, one wrong label means a $300 shoe goes to the wrong person. Chargebacks, angry customers, lost inventory.

What I Built

A unified label management system that connects everything:

  • Connects to StockX and Veeqo APIs — pulls all pending orders into one dashboard
  • Generates pick lists automatically — knows what to grab from the warehouse
  • Downloads all shipping labels in batch — no more clicking through one by one
  • Merges them into a single PDF — organized by location in the warehouse
  • Tracks workflow progress — Pick List → Labels → Download → Complete

The interface shows everything in one place. Select your orders, hit "Download Pick List" to know what to grab, hit "Download Labels" to get everything merged and ready to print.

The Result

3-4 hours → 15 minutes — what took half a day now takes a coffee break
Zero label mix-ups since implementation
Warehouse independence — team can work without waiting on office staff
Scales automatically — whether it's 50 orders or 500
JavaScript REST APIs StockX API Veeqo API PDF Generation Chrome Extension
Case Study 02

Pick Lists & Show Mixes

Intelligent inventory selection that makes live selling shows actually work.

The Problem

We run live selling shows on streaming platforms. Every show needs a "mix"—a selection of products to feature. The old way was chaos:

  1. Manually look through inventory spreadsheet
  2. Try to remember what sold well before
  3. Guess at sizes (do we have smalls? mediums? who knows)
  4. Build a pick list by hand
  5. Walk the warehouse grabbing items
  6. Realize half the stuff is in the wrong spot or already sold

Shows were chaotic. We'd run out of popular sizes mid-stream. We'd grab stuff that was already allocated to other orders. The warehouse was a mess of "where did that go?"

What I Built

An intelligent pick list system with what I call "BAE" (size intelligence) logic:

  • Pulls real-time inventory with complete size breakdown
  • Knows what sizes actually sell — smalls go fast, XXL sits
  • Anti-clustering algorithm — doesn't put all the same brand/style together
  • Warehouse location mapping — generates efficient pick routes
  • Auto-excludes allocated items — nothing that's already promised to another order
  • Creates optimized show mixes — variety, price points, sizes available

The algorithm considers historical sell-through rate by size, current inventory depth, platform-specific preferences, warehouse bin locations, and buffer stock rules. It doesn't just pick random stuff—it picks stuff that will actually sell.

The Result

2 hours → 20 minutes for show prep
40% reduction in "out of stock" moments during live streams
Route-optimized picks — saves walking time in warehouse
Better show conversions because the mix is actually good

Case Study 03

Admin Dashboard & Streamer Portal

Two connected systems that turned team management from chaos into clarity.

The Problem

We have a team of streamers who host live selling shows. Managing them was a nightmare:

  • Schedules in a Google Doc that nobody updated
  • Commission calculations done by hand in a spreadsheet (took 4+ hours weekly)
  • No visibility into performance — who was actually selling?
  • Onboarding docs scattered across email, Drive, Slack
  • Constant questions — "when do I work?" and "how much did I make?"

Management was flying blind. Streamers were frustrated. Payroll was a nightmare every single week.

What I Built

Two connected systems that talk to each other:

Admin Dashboard (internal)

  • Real-time P&L by streamer, by show, by platform
  • Commission engine with custom rules (base rate, performance tiers, bonuses, incentives)
  • Schedule management with conflict detection
  • Performance analytics: units per hour, average sale price, conversion rate
  • Automated payroll reports ready for export

Streamer Portal (employee-facing)

  • Personal dashboard: "Hey [Name]! Here's your performance"
  • See their own stats: units sold, earnings, hours streamed
  • View schedule and submit availability
  • Access training resources and documents
  • Sign required documents (NDA, non-compete) electronically
  • Real-time leaderboard for healthy competition

The commission engine handles complex logic: base hourly rate, per-unit bonuses based on benchmarks, tiered multipliers for hitting targets, special incentive periods, and automatic tip calculations from streams.

The Result

Commission calculation: 4 hours → 10 minutes
Zero payroll errors since launch
Self-service stats — streamers check their own performance instead of asking
Scheduling conflicts caught automatically
60% faster onboarding
Data-driven coaching — finally know who's performing
